Robert Rosskopf

I am the product of many miracles. A miracle is when God interferes with the natural course of human events. I have seen it happen time and time again. My entry into programming as a profession was a miracle. The spirit whispered to me that I should become a programmer. I discovered that I had a natural talent for programming. I bought a popular programming language, and a computer, and challenged myself to write difficult programs. After completing a few, I used the Yellow Pages to call programmers and offer my services. I was hired by a man who eventually sold me to a company where he was a consultant. I found the act of creation to be very satisfying.

Biography

I was born in Santa Monica, California. My father was a life guard and met my mother on the beach. They both converted to the restored gospel, and moved to Salt Lake City, Utah, taking me in tow. Our family was inactive when we moved back to California. My own conversion came after the move, when I read the Book of Mormon, and prayed about it. All of the books at the local public library were critical of the LDS church, The Book of Mormon, and Joseph Smith as a prophet. As I read some of these books, it became clear to me that they had nothing to offer in the way of truth. Since then I have made a study of the thousands of theories and complaints against the church and its beliefs. I have recorded some of my own insights at www.thedewfromheaven.com

My other interests include woodworking, singing, sailing and performance magic.
